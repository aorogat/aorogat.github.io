About

I am a postdoctoral fellow at Concordia University working with Professor Essam Mansour. I completed my PhD in Computer Science at Carleton University under the supervision of Professor Ahmed El-Roby.

My research focuses on multi-agent systems and knowledge-grounded reasoning. I develop architectures that enable intelligent agents to coordinate over structured knowledge graphs for complex question answering, planning, and autonomous decision-making.

Research Interests

  • Multi-agent systems and coordination
  • Knowledge graph question answering
  • Memory-augmented reasoning architectures
  • Benchmark design and systematic evaluation
  • Large language models for structured reasoning

Research

My research addresses fundamental challenges in how AI systems can reason effectively over structured knowledge. I develop methods for decomposing complex reasoning tasks across specialized agents, designing comprehensive evaluation benchmarks, and building scalable knowledge graph systems for real-world applications.

Chatty-KG: Multi-Agent Question Answering over Knowledge Graphs

SIGMOD 2026 (with R. Omar, I. Abdelaziz, O. Mangukiya, P. Kalnis, E. Mansour)

Chatty-KG architecture: multi-agent orchestration with LangGraph for entity retrieval, query planning, and answer validation.

Developed a multi-agent architecture for knowledge graph question answering that decomposes KGQA into specialized reasoning roles. The system uses LangGraph-based orchestration to coordinate entity retrieval, query planning, and answer validation agents, enabling robust multi-step reasoning over large knowledge graphs.

QueryBridge: Large-Scale Dataset for KGQA

CIKM 2025 (with A. El-Roby)

Designed and released a large-scale dataset containing over one million natural language questions paired with executable SPARQL queries. QueryBridge addresses data scarcity in natural language to SPARQL translation research, providing diverse query patterns across multiple knowledge graphs for systematic training and evaluation.

Maestro: Automated Benchmark Generation for KGQA

SIGMOD 2023, VLDB 2022 (with A. El-Roby)

Maestro benchmark generation pipeline: template-driven synthesis, constraint-based instantiation, and difficulty-controlled question generation for KGQA evaluation.

Created an automatic benchmark generation framework that addresses evaluation bias and coverage limitations in knowledge graph question answering. Maestro systematically generates diverse, difficulty-controlled question sets through template-based synthesis and constraint solving, enabling comprehensive and fair evaluation of KGQA systems across multiple dimensions.

Ericsogate: Enterprise Knowledge Graph Platform

CIKM 2024 (with S. L. Vadlamani, D. Thomas, A. El-Roby)

[Six-Layer Architecture]

Data Ingestion → Semantic Modeling → Graph Construction
Query Services → Application Layer

Architected and deployed a production-grade knowledge graph platform for CloudRAN analytics at Ericsson. The system provides semantic search, automated summarization, and retrieval-augmented exploration over heterogeneous operational data.
