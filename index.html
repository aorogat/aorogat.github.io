About

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Computer Science and Software Engineering at Concordia University, where I work with Professor Essam Mansour. I received my PhD in Computer Science from Carleton University under the supervision of Professor Ahmed El-Roby, where my doctoral research focused on the design, evaluation, and large-scale benchmarking of knowledge graph question answering systems.

My research lies at the intersection of multi-agent systems, knowledge representation, and data management. I study how collections of specialized agents can be designed to collaboratively reason over structured knowledge graphs, decompose complex tasks into interpretable subtasks, and maintain persistent memory across long-horizon interactions. A central theme of my work is developing architectures and evaluation methodologies that enable reliable, transparent, and scalable reasoning for knowledge-intensive applications, including complex question answering, planning, and autonomous decision-making.

Research Interests

  • Multi-agent systems and coordination
  • Knowledge graph question answering
  • Memory-augmented reasoning architectures
  • Benchmark design and systematic evaluation
  • Large language models for structured reasoning

Research

My research addresses fundamental challenges in how AI systems can reason effectively over structured knowledge. I develop methods for decomposing complex reasoning tasks across specialized agents, designing comprehensive evaluation benchmarks, and building scalable knowledge graph systems for real-world applications.

Multi-Agent Systems

Research on the design, evaluation, and automation of large language model–based multi-agent systems. This line of work focuses on benchmarking coordination, memory, planning, tool use, and scalability across diverse multi-agent frameworks, as well as enabling natural-language-driven MAS design.

Question Answering over Knowledge Graphs

A comprehensive research program addressing the full pipeline of knowledge graph question answering: from benchmark design and evaluation methodologies to large-scale dataset creation and multi-agent system architectures. This work spans systematic evaluation frameworks, automated benchmark generation, million-scale annotated datasets, and production-ready QA systems.

Chatty-KG: Multi-Agent QA System

SIGMOD 2026

A LangGraph-based multi-agent KGQA system that decomposes reasoning into specialized agents for entity retrieval, query planning, validation, and execution over large knowledge graphs.

QueryBridge: Large-Scale Dataset

1+ million natural language questions paired with executable SPARQL queries. The largest annotated KGQA dataset, enabling research on complex query structures and semantic diversity.

Maestro: Benchmark Generation

Automatic benchmark generation framework using template-driven synthesis and constraint-based instantiation to create diverse and difficulty-controlled KGQA evaluation sets.
