Overview

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Concordia University, where I work with Prof. Essam Mansour on foundational problems at the intersection of multi-agent systems, memory-augmented large language models, and knowledge-rich reasoning. My research investigates how intelligent agents can plan, communicate, and coordinate over complex structured environmentsâ€”particularly when grounded in large-scale knowledge graphs and real-world data ecosystems.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science at Carleton University under the supervision of Prof. Ahmed El-Roby , where my work focused on question answering over knowledge graphs (KGQA), benchmark generation, and the design of scalable, data-centric AI pipelines.

Across both academic and industrial settings, my research explores how large language models can move beyond isolated text generation toward reasoning, retrieval, verification, and autonomous decision-making. I integrate techniques from LLM orchestration, semantic parsing, graph-based retrieval, and multi-agent planning, and I have built end-to-end systems ranging from multi-agent KGQA architectures to enterprise-scale knowledge-graph platforms. My work has appeared in leading venues including SIGMOD, VLDB, and CIKM.

Prior to joining Concordia, I collaborated with Ericsson on the design of production-grade knowledge-graph systems for CloudRAN analytics, including semantic search engines, automated summarization workflows, and retrieval-augmented exploration interfaces. This experience strongly shaped my research perspective: developing AI systems that are not only accurate in controlled benchmarks, but also robust, interpretable, and deployable at scale.

My long-term research goal is to advance memory-aware, multi-agent intelligence capable of structured reasoning, collaborative problem-solving, and continual learning. I study how agents can specialize, form dynamic communication structures, validate each otherâ€™s reasoning processes, and operate reliably in settings where correctness, traceability, and accountability are essential. This line of work supports applications in conversational AI, scientific assistants, autonomous data exploration, and next-generation AI systems equipped with persistent memory and planning capabilities.

Chatty-KG â€” Multi-Agent KGQA System (SIGMOD 2026)

Chatty-KG is a multi-agent question-answering system for knowledge graphs, designed to decompose KGQA into specialised reasoning roles.

The system leverages LangGraph-based agent orchestration, with dedicated agents for entity retrieval, query planning, and answer validation, enabling robust and interpretable multi-step reasoning over large knowledge graphs.

QueryBridge â€” Large-Scale KGQA Dataset (CIKM 2025)

I led the design and release of QueryBridge, a million-scale dataset for knowledge-graph question answering, addressing data scarcity in NL-to-SPARQL research.

The dataset provides over 1M annotated natural-language questions paired with executable SPARQL queries, enabling robust training, evaluation, and analysis of KGQA systems and semantic parsers.

Ericsogate â€” Enterprise Knowledge Graph Platform (CIKM 2024)

Ericsogate is an enterprise-scale knowledge graph platform developed in collaboration with Ericsson to support large-scale analytics, semantic search, and data integration for CloudRAN systems.

The system follows a six-layer architecture covering data ingestion, semantic modeling, graph construction, analytics, and application services. It enables reliable querying, summarization, and exploration of heterogeneous operational data through a unified knowledge graph abstraction.
